Curacha

The curacha is a beloved dish in the Zamboanga region of the Philippines. It is known for its distinct appearance, resembling a cross between a crab and a lobster. Its body is wide, and it has large pincers for claws. Typically, it is cooked in a simple manner, often simmered in spicy coconut milk and various seasonings.

prep time

15 mins

cook time

40mins

serving

4 servings

Tags : Seafood Lunch Dinner intermediate

instructions

step 1

Wash the curacha in running water and use toothbrush to clean the shells if necessary. Drain.

step 2

Put the curacha in a cooking pot or medium size wok. Sprinkle with salt, pour the sprite or 7-up and butter.

step 3

Bring to a boil than simmer until the curacha is cooked and almost all the liquid has evaporated.

step 4

Transfer to a serving dish and serve.

ingredients

1 kilo curacha redfrog crab or spanner crab

2 Tbsp. butter

1 Tbsp. sea salt or rock salt

1 cup sprite or 7-Up

tools

Cooking pot or steamer

Cooking utensils

Measuring tools

Cutting board

Mixing bowls
